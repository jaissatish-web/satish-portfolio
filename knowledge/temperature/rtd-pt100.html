RTD Pt100 Explained

📅 Jan 2025 🏷️ Temperature, RTD, Pt100

The Hook

An RTD at ADNOC showed 10Ω low resistance — suspected broken wire. The real culprit? Lead wire shorted in the marshaling cabinet, bypassing the sensor entirely.

Core Concept

  • Resistance Formula: R = R₀(1 + αT) where α = 0.00385 for Pt100
  • Wiring: 2-wire, 3-wire, 4-wire configurations available
  • Accuracy: ±0.1°C typical, better than thermocouples
  • Self-Heating: Sensor dissipates ~0.1-1W, causes measurement error
  • Lead Wire Compensation: 3-wire RTD compensates for wire resistance

Signal Relationship

Real-World Story

A distillation column temperature measurement at ADNOC kept showing -20°C during startup. Investigation revealed the RTD was reading correctly, but the 3-wire configuration had swapped wires — causing negative temperature readings.

The fix involved removing and reinstalling the RTD with proper wire identification. Always verify wiring diagrams before installation!

🔑 One-Liner Takeaway: RTD resistance changes 0.385Ω/°C — 100Ω at 0°C, 138.5Ω at 100°C, enabling precise temperature measurement.
