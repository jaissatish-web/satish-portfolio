Thermocouple Types

📅 Jan 2025 🏷️ Temperature, Thermocouples, Sensors

The Hook

At NEOM's reformer, a Type K thermocouple failed after 3 months due to sulfur poisoning. Switching to Type R extended service life to 2+ years in the same process.

Core Concept

  • Type K: Chromel-Alumel, -200°C to 1260°C, most common, NOT for sulfur
  • Type J: Iron-Constantan, -40°C to 750°C, iron susceptible to rust
  • Type T: Copper-Constantan, -200°C to 350°C, excellent for moisture
  • Type E: Chromel-Constantan, -200°C to 900°C, highest sensitivity
  • Type N: Nicrosil-Nisil, -200°C to 1300°C, better K alternative
  • Type R/S: Platinum-Rhodium, -50°C to 1700°C, noble metal, expensive
  • Type B: Platinum-Rhodium, 0°C to 1800°C, for ultra-high temp

Real-World Story

NEOM's steam methane reformer used Type K thermocouples for furnace tubes. Within 3 months, multiple sensors drifted low due to sulfur compounds in the process gas attacking the Chromel wire.

The solution was switching to Type S (Platinum-Rhodium) for critical measurements. While initially 5x more expensive, the elimination of unplanned maintenance justified the investment.

🔑 One-Liner Takeaway: Type K is common but fails in sulfur service — use Type N or noble metals for aggressive environments.
