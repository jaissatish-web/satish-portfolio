4-20mA Signal Standard

📅 Jan 2025🏷️ Basics, Signals

The Hook

A transmitter reading 3.8mA doesn't indicate zero process — it signals a broken wire. NEOM's pre-commissioning team caught this before startup.

Core Concept

  • Live Zero: 4mA = 0% span, detects wire breaks
  • Noise Immunity: Current loops resist voltage drop on long runs
  • NAMUR NE43: 3.8-21.2mA = failure indication
  • Standard Range: 4mA = minimum, 20mA = full scale
  • Loop Power: Devices powered from same 24VDC supply

Interactive Simulator

Real-World Story

During ADNOC hydrotesting, a pressure transmitter at 3.5mA was initially thought to be reading low pressure. Investigation revealed water ingress causing leakage current — caught via NE43 diagnostics.

Takeaway: Signal below 4mA always indicates a fault — investigate immediately.
